I am a researcher driven by a deep fascination with how the environment interacts with the genome and epigenome to shape biological outcomes across generations. My early research explored how ecological factors and captivity influence genome-wide variation and epigenetic modifications in natural populations. During my PhD, I used population genomics and epigenomic profiling to study the molecular signatures of environmental stress and domestication, particularly in conservation-relevant fish species. That work deepened my appreciation for how plastic and dynamic the genome can be in response to external cues, and it taught me the value of integrating field-based questions with high-resolution molecular tools.

Building on that foundation, in my postdoctoral work, I focus on how **preconception experiences**—such as stress or environmental exposures—affect DNA methylation in oocytes. I use single-cell methylome sequencing to profile CpG and CpH methylation across individual oocytes and embryos, identifying how maternal environments alter the epigenetic landscape prior to fertilization. My analysis includes clustering cells by epigenetic state, mapping differentially methylated regions and genes, and integrating chromatin state annotations to understand the functional impact of these changes. I am particularly interested in how disrupted or resilient methylation patterns influence early embryonic development and long-term outcomes.

I deeply love the research I do. It gives me the opportunity to ask meaningful questions about biology, work with powerful technologies, and contribute to fields that matters.

Research Projects

  • Preconception Stress and Oocyte Methylation in Primate Models — In my current work, I investigate how preconception stress alters DNA methylation in rhesus macaque oocytes using single-cell whole-genome bisulfite sequencing (scWGBS). I analyze both CpG and CpH methylation across individual oocytes and early embryos, identifying epigenetic biomarkers of stress and developmental potential.
  • Epigenetic and Genomic Basis of Domestication in Delta Smelt — During my PhD, I used WGBS and RAD-seq to study transgenerational epigenetic change in fish reared in conservation hatcheries. My research uncovered methylation signatures associated with domestication selection, helping inform species recovery strategies.
  • Development of Genetic Monitoring Tools for Redband Trout — I co-developed SNP assays and conducted genome-wide analysis to delineate taxonomic boundaries and track population health in endangered Redband Trout populations in California, supporting conservation policy decisions.
  • Genomic Connectivity in Zooplankton Populations — In a separate postdoc, I analyzed genome-wide SNP data to assess zooplankton population structure and connectivity across California water systems, linking ecological patterns to water management strategies.
  • Mito-nuclear Interactions and Oxidative Stress in Drosophila — In earlier work, I studied how mitochondrial and nuclear genome interactions influence fertility and stress response in Drosophila melanogaster, using functional assays and population crosses.
